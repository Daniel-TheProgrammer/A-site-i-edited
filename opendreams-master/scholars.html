Elvis Tanwie

University of Edinburgh

Elvis Tanwie is a Chemical Engineering student at the University of Edinburgh on the prestigious MasterCard Foundation Scholars Program. He works closely with Open Dreams, an educational organization in Cameroon, in assisting high achieving low-income Cameroonians gain access to quality education and was the Project Coordinator for the Open Dreams Summer Academy 2019, Yaoundé. Elvis has a keen interest in global affairs, financial systems and governance.
Miki

University of Sheffield

Miki Founder and CEO of the Vocational Centre for International Development (VCID). He was one of the international delegates to the 2017 First UN World Data Forum and is a consultative member with UN-Habitat on Global Land Tool Indicator (GLII) initiative since 2014. Miki holds a B.Ed in Geography (University of Buea), Certificate in Project Planning and Management (Pan African Institute for Development – West Africa (PAID-WA), Buea), Postgraduate Diploma in Development Management and Governance (PAID-WA, Buea), and M.A in International Development (University of Sheffield, UK). Miki is currently a PhD Student (International and Rural Development) at the University of Reading, UK. His areas of research include: Politics of food security, livelihood dynamics, Political Economy of inequality and geographies of poverty. Miki is a 2012 Chevening Scholar, Open Dreams Pioneer Graduate Scholar and 2019 Commonwealth Scholar. He is currently co-leading the International Development PhD Seminar Network at the School of Agriculture, Policy and Development and is a passionate Social Policy Analyst.
Mikah E. Nuilondea

University of Edinburgh

Mikah E. Nuilondea is a Commonwealth Scholar studying Advanced Mechanical Engineering at Heriot-Watt University Edinburgh. He joined Open Dreams in 2018 after half a decade working as a university student leader helping English-speaking students in Cameroon to overcome barriers and access quality higher educational opportunities through Model Initiative of Africa, an organization be co-founded. I believe that opportunities to education will play a critical role towards Africa’s socio-economic development and a reduction of economic inequalities existing in Africa and is committed to contributing through Open Dreams and Model Initiative of Africa – MIA. As a pioneer Open Dreams graduate scholar, he initiated and currently coordinates the graduate department of Open Dreams which focuses on supporting postgraduate students access further study opportunities, conferences and to foster international cooperation with their peers.
